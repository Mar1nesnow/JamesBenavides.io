About

a little about my background, education, and where I've worked.

I'm an environmental scientist with a passion for understanding ecosystems through both data and lived experience.

In 2025, I earned dual degrees in Biology (Marine, Ecology, and Evolution) and Gender, Sexuality, and Women's Studies from Bowdoin College in Maine — an interdisciplinary foundation that shapes how I approach environmental work as both science and story.

My research experience spans sea star behavior, oyster aquaculture, and marine protist culturing, with experience designing, conducting, and presenting projects. With Texas Parks and Wildlife — Coastal Fisheries, I worked on permitting, public engagement, and fisheries sampling, gaining insight into applied conservation at the state level.

I currently serve as an AmeriCorps member at Whiterock Conservancy in Iowa, focused on land stewardship — invasive species removal, soil and water sampling, environmental education, and trail and campground maintenance.

I value preparation and safety in the field, but I also believe curiosity and connection to place are just as essential. Moving forward, I'm eager to deepen my skills in data analysis, programming, scientific communication, and grant writing alongside hands-on environmental work.

Skills & Expertise

the tools, techniques, and field methods I bring to land stewardship, coastal fisheries, and marine research.

Land & Fire Management
Habitat Restoration & Stewardship

Conservation work requires equal parts ecology and practical problem-solving. My experience includes restoring native habitats, maintaining public recreation areas, and improving ecosystem health across prairies, woodlands, and riparian corridors.

  • Mechanical and chemical control of invasive species
  • Chainsaw felling and brush cutting of invasive shrubs and trees
  • Herbicide application for stump treatment and girdling
  • Skid steer operation for moving logs and woody debris
  • Timber stand improvement to enhance forest structure
  • Trail mowing, campground maintenance, and public access improvements
Prescribed Fire

Prescribed fire is one of the most effective tools for maintaining healthy prairie ecosystems. I've assisted with burn operations from planning and preparation through active ignition and post-burn assessment.

  • Firebreak preparation and equipment staging
  • Drip torch operation during prescribed burns
  • Holding and monitoring fire lines
  • Smoke and weather condition monitoring
  • Post-burn vegetation assessments
Coastal & Marine Research
Fisheries Field Methods

My fisheries experience comes from estuarine monitoring in Texas, where fieldwork meant adapting to changing tides, weather, and sampling conditions while collecting reliable biological data.

  • Bag seining for juvenile fish and invertebrate communities
  • Side-scan sonar surveys for benthic habitat mapping
  • Quadrat sampling for oyster reef and benthic assessments
  • Offshore vertical longline deployment and retrieval
  • Water quality monitoring using YSI multiparameter sondes
  • Small vessel operation in estuarine environments
Oyster Aquaculture Research

My aquaculture research focused on evaluating whether natural tidal energy could serve as a low-cost alternative to mechanical tumbling for eastern oyster production.

  • Designed field experiments comparing intertidal and subtidal culture methods
  • Monitored oyster growth, survival, and shell morphology
  • Quantified tidal exposure and environmental conditions
  • Collected and analyzed biological datasets
  • Integrated Indigenous Knowledge and Western science through a Two-Eyed Seeing framework
Marine Ecology Research

My research spans multiple marine species and ecological questions, from shellfish behavior to predator-prey dynamics.

  • Scallop swimming and orientation responses to light

  • Oyster growth and performance in aquaculture systems
  • Long-term environmental monitoring in coastal ecosystems
  • Experimental design and hypothesis development
  • Animal husbandry for oysters, scallops, and sea stars
  • Daily monitoring of flow-through seawater systems
  • Behavioral observation and ecological data collection
