About ⋆.˙.

Where I've been. What I do. Where I want to go!

Howdy! I'm James Benavides.

I grew up on the Gulf of Mexico, where my love for the natural world began. Exploring coastal ecosystems, collecting shells on the beach, and observing the incredible diversity of marine life sparked a fascination with biology that eventually led me to earn a degree in it.

My passion for the environment has taken me from Texas → Maine → North Carolina → Denmark → Iowa. Along the way, I have developed research projects focused on oyster aquaculture, sea star ecology, and scallop behavior.

But science doesn't stop in the lab. My experiences in the field have been just as important. Working in coastal fisheries in Texas strengthened my skills in boat operation and fisheries sampling techniques, while land stewardship in Iowa has given me hands-on experience with equipment such as chainsaws, the Ditch Witch, and mowers.

Community is at the heart of everything I do. Whether I'm designing research projects that address community needs or leading environmental education programs for students and adults, I believe that meaningful work should benefit both people and the environment.

Currently, I am serving an 11-month AmeriCorps term at Whiterock Conservancy in Coon Rapids, Iowa — and I'm loving the Midwest.

Outside of work, I'm always looking to improve my birding and fishing skills. Birds I can watch, fish I can eat...what more could you ask for?

Field / Lab Skills

Land Stewardship
restoring native prairie, woodland, and riparian habitats

This work involves reading a habitat and determining the best course of action for that space, most often through timber stand improvement and invasive species removal.

Tackling invasive honeysuckle, for example, has been a large part of my work, but whether to foliar spray or hack-and-squirt depends on the time of year and what surrounds the target plant. Thinning trees that are too dense also requires an understanding of spacing that distinguishes a savanna from a woodland.

Mapping Soil Properties
2026
Whiterock Conservancy · Coon Rapids, Iowa

Whiterock Conservancy manages 5,500 acres in central Iowa, where soil health shapes decisions across conservation, recreation, and agriculture. Understanding what's in the ground — and how it behaves — is foundational to good land management.

I developed an interactive mapping system that characterizes soil texture, organic matter, erodibility, drainage, and saturated hydraulic conductivity across the full Whiterock boundary. I also consolidated three years of farm soil sampling results and made them browsable year by year within the same map.

The project drew on SQL queries to pull soil attributes from the USDA NRCS Web Soil Survey, with ArcGIS Online used to join datasets to shapefile boundaries and build the final presentation layer.
